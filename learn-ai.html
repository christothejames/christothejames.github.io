A field guide

Talk to AI like you mean it.

Most people get mediocre answers from AI because they ask mediocre questions. The fix isn't a secret trick — it's three habits, shown below.

prompt.txt
write me something about dogs write a 150-word Instagram caption about my rescue greyhound's first time seeing snow — playful tone, one emoji max
→ this version gets you a caption you'd actually post. the first one gets you generic dog facts.

The three switches

These aren't steps to follow in order — they're dials you can turn on any prompt, any time, to get a sharper answer.

Be specific

Swap "something about X" for the actual thing you need: length, audience, tone, purpose. Vague in, vague out.

Give context

AI can't see your inbox, your codebase, or your situation. Paste the relevant bits in, or explain the constraint that matters.

Set the format

Say how you want the answer shaped: a table, three bullet points, plain prose, code only. Don't make it guess.

What trips people up

The most common habits that quietly limit what AI can do for you.

  • 01

    Stopping at the first answer. Treat the reply as a draft, not a verdict — tell it what's off and let it revise.

  • 02

    Not defining "good." If you know what a great answer looks like, say so up front instead of hoping it guesses.

  • 03

    Assuming it remembers your situation. Unless you've told it in this conversation, it doesn't know your job, your codebase, or your constraints.

  • 04

    Asking one giant question. Big, tangled requests get muddled answers. Break it into the pieces you'd explain to a colleague one at a time.
